    Most Successful Disney Movie Analysis

    This project set out to answer the question: What makes the most successful Disney movie? We took a look at a representative example of Disney movies to answer this question. Success is a metric defined by movies that earned the most inflation adjusted income. We analyzed the data to isolate specific characteristics of movies in order to find the most revenue generating combinations.

    Key Takeaways

    A main key takeaway that we got from this data is that the characteristics that made a movie successful changed throughout time. Continuously changing cultural trends and media interests changes what makes a movie popular.

    Through the Decades

    The key takeaway from this section is that the most successful decades were the 1930s and 1940s, which were followed by a steady decline before they increased again in the modern day. Movies in the earlier decades met success with their G rated Musicals and Adventure movies. Decreasing revenue in the decades past caused Disney to pivot to focusing more on producing PG and G adventure movies, which helped increase their revenue once more.

    Movie Logistics

    The key takeaway from this section is that the most successful movies had the following logistics: Adventure PG movies released during the holiday months. Movies with one or more of these logistical characteristics generated more gross revenue.

    About the Movie

    The key takeaway from this section is that the most successful Disney movies are structured to include songs and both a hero and a villain. Throughout the decades, these features trademarked success time and time again. Additionally, in a high revenue generating genre, Adventure was structured in a way to include a characteristic associated with success: having both a Hero & a Villain.
