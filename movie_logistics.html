    Movie Logisitics

    The primary goal of this section is to answer the question: What logistics make the most successful movie? The following figures look at some logistical aspect of the movie to give key insights.

    Release Month Verus Income, Showing Best Release Month

    The figure depicts the average inflation-adjusted income of movies released in specific months of the year. The goal of this figure is to look at what role a specific movie logistic, month released, plays in movie success. Each number on the x-axis corresponds to the respective month (e.g. 2 is February). The three months with the most gross income are February, December, and October, and the three months with the lowest gross income were January, August, and September. One key detail about the highest income grossing months is that they all occur around major holidays, while the lowest income grossing months are during months that are not associated with major holiday seasons. This figure indicates that the most successful month to release a movie is around a major holiday season, such as Christmas, Valentine’s day, and Halloween.

    Title Length Versus Gross Income

    The figure depicts the average inflation-adjusted income of movies released in specific months of the year. The goal of this figure is to look at what role a specific movie logistic, length of movie title, plays in movie success. The figure depicts no correlation between the length of a movie title and the gross income that it generates. The figure suggests that title length is a movie logistic that does not play a role in movie success.
